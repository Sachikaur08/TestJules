Mental Health Assessment Questionnaire

Instructions: Please read each question carefully and select the answer that best describes your feelings and experiences over the past two weeks.

Section 1: Mood

1. How often have you been bothered by feeling down, depressed, or hopeless?

2. How often have you been bothered by little interest or pleasure in doing things?

Section 2: Anxiety

3. How often have you been bothered by feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge?

4. How often have you been bothered by not being able to stop or control worrying?

Section 3: Stress

5. How often have you felt that you were unable to control the important things in your life?

6. How often have you felt confident about your ability to handle your personal problems?

Section 4: Coping Mechanisms

7. When you're faced with a stressful situation, how often do you engage in activities you enjoy to feel better?

8. How often do you feel you have someone you can talk to about your problems?

Section 5: Overall Well-being

9. Overall, how would you rate your quality of life?

10. How often have you felt energetic and full of life?

Important Considerations

Purpose of this Tool: This questionnaire is intended as a self-assessment tool to help you gain general insight into your current mental well-being. It is for informational and educational purposes only.

Not a Diagnostic Tool: The results of this questionnaire are not a clinical diagnosis. This tool does not replace a consultation with a qualified mental health professional or a medical doctor. If you have concerns about your mental health, please seek advice from a healthcare provider.

Data Privacy: Your responses to this questionnaire are processed locally in your web browser. No data is sent to any server, and your responses are not stored or tracked by this tool after you close this page.

Seeking Help: If your results cause you concern, or if you are feeling distressed, it's important to reach out for support. Consider talking to a trusted friend or family member, your doctor, or a mental health professional. Many countries have mental health hotlines available. For example, you can search online for "mental health hotline [your country/region]" or "find a therapist [your area]".

In an emergency, please contact your local emergency services immediately.
