Question 1 (Easy): What is the SI unit of electric charge?

The SI unit of electric charge is the Coulomb (C).

Question 2 (Medium): Two charges of 10C and -15C are separated by a distance of 2m. What is the force between them?

Using Coulomb's Law, F = k * |q1*q2| / r^2 = (8.987 x 10^9) * |10 * -15| / 2^2 = 3.375 x 10^12 N. Since the charges are opposite, the force is attractive.

Question 3 (Hard): A charge Q is divided into two parts, q and Q-q. What is the ratio Q/q for the force between the two parts to be maximum?

For the force to be maximum, the derivative of the force with respect to q must be zero. This occurs when q = Q/2, so Q/q = 2.
