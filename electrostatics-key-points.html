Key Points

  • Electric charge is quantized, meaning it exists in discrete units.
  • Like charges repel, and opposite charges attract.
  • Coulomb's Law describes the force between two point charges.
  • The electric field is a vector field that describes the electric force exerted on a positive test charge at any given point.
  • Electric potential is the work done per unit charge to move a positive test charge from infinity to a given point in an electric field.

Formulas

  • Coulomb’s Law: F = 1/(4πε₀) * q₁q₂/r²
  • Electric Field: E = F/q
  • Electric Flux: ΦE = E⋅A = EAcosθ
  • Gauss’s Law: ΦE = q_in/ε₀
  • Electric field due to infinite line charge: E = λ/(2πε₀r)
  • Electric field due to uniformly charged sphere: E = 1/(4πε₀) * Q/r²
